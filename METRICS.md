# Credex Spend Audit - Growth & Product Metrics Handbook
This handbook establishes the product instrumentation framework, tracking metrics, and conversion funnels to align the Credex platform around a single unified target: **Total Activated Savings (TAS)**.

---

## 1. The North Star Metric: Total Activated Savings (TAS)
* **Definition:** The aggregate annual cash savings (in USD) successfully actualized by startups who completed an audit on Credex and executed our recommended downgrades, cancellations, or API caching optimizations.
* **Why it matters:** It directly measures the real-world financial impact Credex delivers. Unlike superficial metrics like "pageviews" or "registered leads," TAS represents hard, defensible capital returned to startup runways, verifying our product-market fit.

---

## 2. Key Input Metrics
To drive the North Star metric, the product squad monitors four core input metrics across the user lifecycle:

```mermaid
funnel
    title Credex Conversion Funnel
    AuditsStarted : 100%
    AuditsCompleted : 75%
    LeadsCaptured : 30%
    SavingsConsultationsBooked : 12.5%
```

1. **Audits Started (Top of Funnel):** The daily volume of unique visitors who click "Run Free Infrastructure Audit" or toggle an active tool in the grid stack.
2. **Audit Completion Rate (Mid-Funnel Friction):** The percentage of users who start the audit and progress all the way through Step 3 to view their calculated results.
   * *Target:* $\ge 75\%$ completion rate.
3. **Lead Capture Conversion Rate (Gating efficiency):** The percentage of high-saving users who submit their company profiles (email, company name, role) to unlock their personalized spend summary.
   * *Target:* $\ge 30\%$ conversion.
4. **Viral Share Multiplier (K-Factor):** The average number of new audit visits generated by each shared Base64 URL card.
   * *Formula:* $K = (\text{Shared Links Created}) \times (\text{Clicks per Shared Link}) \times (\text{Conversion Rate of new traffic})$
   * *Target:* $K \ge 0.15$ (signifying strong referral loops in founder Slacks).

---

## 3. Product Instrumentation & Event Schema
We track five critical event boundaries using simple client-side hooks:
* `audit_funnel_started`: Fired when Step 1 is loaded.
* `tool_grid_toggled`: Tracked when tools are selected/deselected in Step 2.
* `audit_results_compiled`: Logged when Step 3 calculates savings.
* `lead_gating_unlocked`: Triggered when lead capture forms are successfully submitted.
* `report_link_copied`: Logged when a user copies the Base64 shareable link to their clipboard.

---

## 4. Key Pivot Decisions
If input metrics drift from their target baselines, we execute structured product pivots:
* **High Completion Rate / Low Lead Capture:** Indicates gating is too restrictive. **Action:** Pivot from a strict lead-gate to a "soft-gate" where users can view 80% of their results but must submit their email only to claim the startup credits application guides.
* **Low Viral Share Multiplier (K-Factor):** Indicates share CTAs are uncompelling. **Action:** Refactor `share/page.tsx` to include one-click "Share to Twitter/X" and "Post to Slack" buttons with dynamic visual saving badges.
